## Briefing

### Product

ALR-217, an oral small-molecule inhibitor developed by Aurelis Therapeutics. Once-daily oral administration; outpatient setting compatible.

### Indication

Relapsed/refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) in patients ineligible for or progressing after CAR-T cell therapy. Pivotal Phase 2 single-arm readout reported.

### Lifecycle Stage

Post-Phase 2 / pre-launch. Anticipated regulatory submission in the next 12 months; commercial availability targeted ~18 months from briefing date. Long-term follow-up and the in-flight real-world evidence study will read out post-launch.

### Audiences

Primary: practicing hematologist-oncologists in community and academic settings. Secondary: academic key opinion leaders in lymphoma; pharmacy & therapeutics committees at large integrated delivery networks (IDNs); payer medical directors as a tertiary audience for the value-framework pillar.

### Strategic Priorities

1. Differentiate ALR-217 from emerging bispecific antibodies on convenience, sequencing flexibility, and tolerability in older patients.
2. Establish ALR-217's optimal positioning in the 3rd-line and beyond setting given the post-CAR-T treatment landscape.
3. Communicate the safety profile credibly to community oncologists, including practical management of expected adverse events.

### Competitive Context

Two principal competitor classes in the 3rd-line+ DLBCL setting: bispecific antibodies (intravenous, infusion-center delivered, with cytokine-release-syndrome and neurotoxicity monitoring requirements) and alternative oral small-molecule agents. The bispecific class is the more visible competitive frame given the volume of ASH and EHA presentations; the oral-agent comparator is the more relevant operational comparison for community use. Cross-trial comparisons are limited by single-arm pivotal designs across the class.

### Pillar P-01: unmet-need

#### Strategic Rationale

The Unmet Need pillar establishes the medical reality of r/r DLBCL after CAR-T or in CAR-T-ineligible patients — the population where ALR-217 is positioned. It anchors the platform's framing of post-CAR-T treatment as a setting with limited durable options and substantial operational constraints. It speaks primarily to practicing hematologist-oncologists who recognize the clinical pressure of post-CAR-T progression, and provides the medical-need foundation that downstream pillars (P-04 Clinical Evidence, P-05 Clinical Value Framework) build upon.

#### Narrative

CAR-T cell therapy transformed outcomes for many patients with r/r DLBCL, but a substantial fraction either does not achieve durable remission with CAR-T or is not eligible for CAR-T at presentation. The post-CAR-T progression population faces limited remaining options: bispecific antibodies require specialized infusion infrastructure and active monitoring for cytokine-release syndrome and neurotoxicity; chemotherapy regimens at this point in the disease trajectory yield short-duration responses; clinical-trial access is concentrated at academic centers. Older patients and patients in community-treatment settings — a demographic disproportionately represented in the post-CAR-T population — face additional access barriers tied to monitoring requirements and travel for infusion administration. The unmet need at this point in the treatment journey is for active, tolerable, operationally feasible therapy that fits the patient and the care setting.

#### Scope

In scope: the medical-need framing for r/r DLBCL after CAR-T or in CAR-T-ineligible patients; access constraints associated with current options; the demographic profile of the post-CAR-T population.

Out of scope (deferred to other pillars): mechanism-of-action exposition for ALR-217 specifically (P-03 Mechanism of Action); clinical-evidence presentation for ALR-217 (P-04 Clinical Evidence); pricing, value, or positioning recommendations (P-05 Clinical Value Framework); disease pathophysiology beyond what frames the unmet need (P-02 Disease Mechanism).

## Lexicon

### older patients

- preferred: older patients
- avoid: elderly patients, geriatric patients, the elderly
- definition: Patients aged 65 years or older, used in the context of subgroup analyses or operational considerations for community treatment.
- rationale: "Older patients" is the preferred construction in current oncology publishing per ASH and ASCO style guidance; "elderly" is increasingly avoided as paternalistic.

### relapsed/refractory

- preferred: relapsed/refractory
- avoid: relapsed and refractory, R/R, R&R
- definition: The clinical category of DLBCL in which patients have either relapsed after a prior response or never achieved a response (refractory) to a prior therapy line.
- rationale: The slash form is the convention in lymphoma literature; abbreviations introduce ambiguity for non-specialist readers in P&T audiences.

### post-CAR-T

- preferred: post-CAR-T
- avoid: after CAR-T, following CAR-T cell therapy, post-CAR T-cell
- definition: The treatment landscape and patient population sequenced after CAR-T cell therapy administration, whether by progression after CAR-T or by ineligibility.
- rationale: Hyphenated compound is the convention in current lymphoma reviews and matches ALR-217's pivotal study population framing.

## Style Guide

### Voice and Tone

Dry scientific register throughout. Audiences include practicing hematologist-oncologists and academic key opinion leaders, both of whom expect publication-style exposition; payer and P&T audiences receive the same register with additional contextualization rather than a softer register.

### Sentence Construction

Sentences may be long where the science is dense; short for short's sake is disallowed (see Disallowed Patterns 4). Paragraph structure follows the standard publication convention: topic sentence, evidence, qualification.

### Disallowed Patterns

The following patterns are disallowed by default in pilar drafted copy. They codify the dry-scientific-register requirements in §9 of the natural-language spec. The Editor enforces these defaults when running on drafted artifacts. Clients may override individual patterns via the **Overrides** subsection below; an override must specify *which pattern* and *why*.

1. **Em dashes used for rhythmic effect.** Em dashes are permitted only for genuine parenthetical insertions, not for stylistic pacing.
2. **Antithetical constructions of the form "it is not X, it is Y".** Disallowed regardless of whether X and Y are factually accurate. The construction is rhetorical, not scientific.
3. **Sentence-initial conjunctions used for rhetorical pacing.** "But," "And," "So," at sentence start are not allowed for emphasis. Internal conjunctions are unaffected.
4. **Short punchy sentences used for emphasis.** Single-clause stylistic punches that interrupt scientific exposition.
5. **Hedging phrases that soften factual claims without scientific reason.** Hedging is permitted — required, even — when it reflects genuine evidentiary uncertainty. Hedging without that justification is not.
6. **Marketing-register adjectives.** "Robust," "compelling," "groundbreaking," "transformative," "best-in-class," "novel," and similar register markers.
7. **First-person plural framing of the science.** "We see…", "Our data show…" — the science is the subject, not the speakers.
8. **Rhetorical questions.** Particularly in introductory or transitional positions; also disallowed inline.

### Citation Conventions

Vancouver style for the platform's reference list. Inline citations as `[REF-NNN]` during pilar drafting; translation to Vancouver-numbered superscripts is downstream of pilar (§11). Congress abstracts cited with year and abstract number; peer-reviewed publications cited with full author list (or first author + et al if >6 authors), journal, year, volume, pages.

### Evidence Description Conventions

Study design named explicitly (single-arm Phase 2; randomized Phase 3; retrospective real-world cohort) on first invocation per pillar. Primary endpoints distinguished from exploratory or secondary endpoints. Numerical results reported with confidence intervals; medians with ranges. Hedging language calibrated to evidence strength: single-arm data warrant cross-trial-comparison cautions; descriptive comparisons across non-randomized studies are not framed as comparative effectiveness.

### Other

The platform deliberately limits cross-trial numerical comparisons to descriptive context per the Evidence Description Conventions above. Quantitative comparisons across single-arm studies are not made; the platform may flag where such comparisons would be inappropriate.
