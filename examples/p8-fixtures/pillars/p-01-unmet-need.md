# Pillar: Unmet Need

## Strategic Rationale

The Unmet Need pillar establishes the medical reality of r/r DLBCL after CAR-T or in CAR-T-ineligible patients — the population where ALR-217 is positioned. It anchors the platform's framing of post-CAR-T treatment as a setting with limited durable options and substantial operational constraints. It speaks primarily to practicing hematologist-oncologists who recognize the clinical pressure of post-CAR-T progression, and provides the medical-need foundation that downstream pillars (P-04 Clinical Evidence, P-05 Clinical Value Framework) build upon.

## Narrative

CAR-T cell therapy transformed outcomes for many patients with r/r DLBCL, but a substantial fraction either does not achieve durable remission with CAR-T or is not eligible for CAR-T at presentation. The post-CAR-T progression population faces limited remaining options: bispecific antibodies require specialized infusion infrastructure and active monitoring for cytokine-release syndrome and neurotoxicity; chemotherapy regimens at this point in the disease trajectory yield short-duration responses; clinical-trial access is concentrated at academic centers. Older patients and patients in community-treatment settings — a demographic disproportionately represented in the post-CAR-T population — face additional access barriers tied to monitoring requirements and travel for infusion administration. The unmet need at this point in the treatment journey is for active, tolerable, operationally feasible therapy that fits the patient and the care setting.

## Scope

In scope: the medical-need framing for r/r DLBCL after CAR-T or in CAR-T-ineligible patients; access constraints associated with current options; the demographic profile of the post-CAR-T population.

Out of scope (deferred to other pillars): mechanism-of-action exposition for ALR-217 specifically (P-03 Mechanism of Action); clinical-evidence presentation for ALR-217 (P-04 Clinical Evidence); pricing, value, or positioning recommendations (P-05 Clinical Value Framework); disease pathophysiology beyond what frames the unmet need (P-02 Disease Mechanism).

## Scientific Statements

### SS-01: Post-CAR-T progression population

- status: approved
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

**Statement.** A substantial fraction of patients with r/r DLBCL who receive CAR-T cell therapy progress within the first year, and a separate fraction is ineligible for CAR-T at presentation; together these populations constitute the post-CAR-T treatment setting where active, tolerable options are limited.

**Strategic Argument.** Defines the patient population for whom ALR-217 is being positioned. Without a clear articulation of the medical setting, downstream evidence and value claims have no audience-relevant anchoring. Advances Strategic Priority 1 (older-patient differentiation) by establishing the demographic context that bispecific-comparison framings rely on.

**Reference Statements.**

#### RS-01: Treatment-guideline positioning of post-CAR-T options

- status: approved
- sources: [Roe_K_2024_Synth-Clin-Oncol]
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

Treatment guidelines for B-cell lymphomas describe the 3rd-line and post-CAR-T setting as one of evolving evidence with multiple emerging classes; oral small-molecule agents and bispecific antibodies are both recognized in current guideline language as appropriate options for selected patients. The guideline excerpt acknowledges that the choice of regimen depends on patient and operational factors that are not fully prescribed by the guidelines themselves.

### SS-02: Operational constraints in current post-CAR-T options

- status: approved
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

**Statement.** Current bispecific antibody options for post-CAR-T r/r DLBCL require infusion infrastructure, active monitoring for cytokine-release syndrome and neurotoxicity, and care-team availability that is not uniformly accessible across community oncology settings.

**Strategic Argument.** Establishes the operational dimension of the unmet need that ALR-217's oral, outpatient profile addresses. Advances Strategic Priority 1 (operational differentiation) and Strategic Priority 3 (community-oncologist relevance) by making the operational constraints concrete from the perspective of the treating physician and the care team.

**Reference Statements.**

#### RS-01: Bispecific monitoring requirements

- status: approved
- sources: [Doe_AB_2023_Synth-Lab-Med]
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

Pivotal bispecific antibody data for r/r DLBCL describe monitoring protocols for cytokine-release syndrome and neurotoxicity that include in-patient or extended outpatient observation during initial dosing, with documented incidence rates of grade ≥2 CRS and immune effector cell-associated neurotoxicity syndrome that warrant active management. The infrastructure and care-team requirements are non-trivial for community treatment settings.

#### RS-02: Guideline acknowledgment of access variability

- status: approved
- sources: [Roe_K_2024_Synth-Clin-Oncol]
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

Current B-cell lymphoma guideline language acknowledges that access to specialized infusion-administered therapies and to clinical trials varies across treatment settings, and that the choice among 3rd-line options is appropriately individualized to patient circumstances and care-setting capabilities.
