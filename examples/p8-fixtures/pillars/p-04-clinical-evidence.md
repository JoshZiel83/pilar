# Pillar: Clinical Evidence

## Strategic Rationale

The Clinical Evidence pillar establishes ALR-217's efficacy and tolerability profile in r/r DLBCL, anchoring the platform's claims of differentiation against bispecific antibodies on convenience and tolerability while acknowledging the limitations of single-arm data. It directly advances Strategic Priority 1 (differentiation against bispecific antibodies on convenience, sequencing flexibility, and tolerability) and Strategic Priority 3 (credible safety communication to community oncologists). It speaks primarily to practicing hematologist-oncologists and academic key opinion leaders; the value-framework pillar reuses these findings in audience-tailored positioning.

## Narrative

Pivotal Phase 2 data demonstrate clinically meaningful activity in patients with r/r DLBCL who are ineligible for or have progressed after CAR-T, with a tolerability profile dominated by manageable cytopenias and infections rather than the cytokine-release-syndrome and neurotoxicity patterns characteristic of T-cell engaging therapies. The single-arm design and the absence of a randomized comparator are acknowledged; long-term follow-up and the in-flight real-world evidence study will further characterize durability and effectiveness in routine practice. Subgroup analyses, including elderly patients, support consistent activity across patient profiles likely to be encountered in community practice.

The clinical evidence supports an oral-administration option in 3rd-line and beyond, where the operational reality of bispecific infusion infrastructure, monitoring requirements, and adverse-event burden may constrain access for elderly or community-treated patients. Treatment-guideline positioning of oral agents as appropriate alternatives or sequencing options in this setting reinforces the platform's framing.

## Scope

In scope: pivotal Phase 2 efficacy and safety findings; subgroup analyses from the pivotal study; comparative descriptive context from competitor publications where directly relevant to safety or operational differentiation; treatment-guideline positioning of oral agents in 3rd-line.

Out of scope (deferred to other pillars): mechanism-of-action exposition (P-03 Mechanism of Action), broader unmet-need framing (P-01 Unmet Need), treatment-sequencing recommendations and value framing (P-05 Clinical Value Framework), and disease-pathophysiology exposition (P-02 Disease Mechanism).

## Scientific Statements

### SS-01: Pivotal Phase 2 efficacy in r/r DLBCL

- status: approved
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

**Statement.** ALR-217 demonstrated clinically meaningful activity in adults with r/r DLBCL who were ineligible for or had progressed after CAR-T cell therapy, with response rates supporting its role as an oral option in the 3rd-line and beyond setting.

**Strategic Argument.** Establishes the proof-of-concept evidentiary foundation for the platform. Without supportable efficacy claims grounded in the pivotal study, downstream differentiation and value claims are unanchored. The single-arm design is hedged appropriately rather than overclaimed (see GAP-002 for the comparative-data gap).

#### RS-02: Single-arm context relative to bispecific antibody data

- status: approved
- sources: [REF-001, REF-002]
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

The ALR-217 pivotal study and the principal bispecific antibody pivotal data in this setting are both single-arm in design and not directly comparable. Cross-trial differences in patient populations, prior-therapy histories, and assessment criteria limit numerical comparison; clinically meaningful comparisons of efficacy await randomized data or appropriately analyzed real-world evidence.

### SS-02: Tolerability and adverse-event profile

- status: approved
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

**Statement.** ALR-217's adverse-event profile in the pivotal Phase 2 study was dominated by manageable cytopenias and infections, with an absence of grade ≥3 cardiac signals and a profile distinct from the cytokine-release-syndrome and neurotoxicity patterns characteristic of T-cell engaging therapies in this setting.

**Strategic Argument.** Tolerability is a primary differentiation axis for older patients and community-treated patients per Strategic Priority 1; credible communication of expected adverse events and their management is also Strategic Priority 3. The differentiated AE profile is operationally important for community oncologists who manage these patients without dedicated infusion infrastructure or on-call neurology consultation.

**Reference Statements.**

#### RS-01: Adverse events reported in the pivotal study

- status: approved
- sources: [REF-001]
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

The most common adverse events reported in the ALR-217 pivotal Phase 2 study were manageable cytopenias and infection. No grade ≥3 cardiac signals were identified at the primary analysis. Specific incidence rates and management strategies are reported in the primary publication.

#### RS-02: Operational implications for community treatment settings

- status: approved
- sources: [REF-001, REF-003]
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

The oral-administration profile and the absence of CRS or grade ≥3 neurotoxicity signals reduce monitoring and infrastructure requirements relative to bispecific infusion regimens. Treatment-guideline positioning of oral agents as appropriate alternatives or sequencing options in r/r DLBCL after CAR-T or in CAR-T-ineligible patients reinforces the operational case for community use.
