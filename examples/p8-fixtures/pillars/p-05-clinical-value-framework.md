# Pillar: Clinical Value Framework

## Strategic Rationale

The Clinical Value Framework pillar synthesizes the clinical, operational, and quality-of-life dimensions of ALR-217's profile into a value framing for academic key opinion leaders, P&T committees, and payer audiences. It speaks to Strategic Priority 1 (operational and tolerability differentiation) by making the value implications of differentiated tolerability concrete, and to Strategic Priority 3 (community-oncologist credibility) by tying value framing to the realities of community treatment.

## Narrative

Clinical value in r/r DLBCL after CAR-T comprises multiple dimensions: efficacy on disease-relevant endpoints, tolerability for patients managing comorbidity and prior-therapy burden, operational fit for the care setting, and patient-experience implications including outpatient delivery and reduced monitoring burden. ALR-217's profile — oral administration, manageable cytopenia and infection profile, absence of cytokine-release-syndrome and neurotoxicity signals — distributes value across these dimensions in a pattern distinct from the bispecific antibody class. The framework structures the value conversation for audiences who need to weigh differentiated dimensions of clinical and operational value rather than head-to-head efficacy comparisons (which the single-arm evidence base does not support).

## Scope

In scope: the framework dimensions for evaluating clinical value in r/r DLBCL post-CAR-T; how ALR-217's profile distributes across those dimensions; framing for P&T and payer audiences.

Out of scope (deferred to other pillars): pivotal evidence presentation (P-04 Clinical Evidence); medical-need framing (P-01 Unmet Need); mechanism-of-action exposition (P-03); pricing strategy (out of scope for the platform per briefing).

## Scientific Statements

### SS-01: Multi-dimensional value framework for post-CAR-T therapy

- status: approved
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

**Statement.** Clinical value in r/r DLBCL after CAR-T is appropriately evaluated across efficacy, tolerability, operational fit, and patient-experience dimensions; in the absence of head-to-head randomized comparisons across emerging classes, this multi-dimensional framing is the appropriate basis for treatment-selection conversations with patients and care teams.

**Strategic Argument.** Establishes the analytic frame the platform uses for value claims. Without an explicit framework, value claims devolve into single-axis efficacy comparisons that the single-arm evidence base does not support. Aligns with the Evidence Description Conventions in the style guide that disallow cross-trial numerical comparisons.

Current B-cell lymphoma treatment guidelines acknowledge that treatment selection in r/r DLBCL after CAR-T is shaped by patient factors, prior-therapy history, care-setting capabilities, and patient preferences, in addition to efficacy considerations. The guideline language supports framing treatment choice across multiple dimensions rather than as a single comparative-efficacy decision.

### SS-02: ALR-217's value distribution across framework dimensions

- status: approved
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

**Statement.** ALR-217's clinical profile — oral administration, manageable cytopenia and infection profile, absence of grade ≥3 cardiac signals, and absence of the cytokine-release-syndrome and neurotoxicity patterns characteristic of T-cell engaging therapies — distributes value across the framework dimensions in a pattern that prioritizes tolerability and operational fit alongside efficacy.

**Strategic Argument.** Translates the clinical-evidence pillar's findings into value language for P&T and payer audiences. Advances Strategic Priority 1 (tolerability and operational differentiation) by making the value implications of those differentiation axes explicit.

**Reference Statements.**

#### RS-01: Clinical profile underpinning the value distribution

- status: approved
- sources: [Smith_J_2024_Synth-J-Oncol]
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

The pivotal Phase 2 study of ALR-217 in r/r DLBCL after ≥2 prior lines reported a tolerability profile dominated by manageable cytopenias and infection, with no grade ≥3 cardiac signals at the primary analysis. The oral once-daily dosing supports outpatient administration without infusion-center infrastructure.

#### RS-02: Comparative operational profile

- status: approved
- sources: [Smith_J_2024_Synth-J-Oncol, Doe_AB_2023_Synth-Lab-Med]
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

The operational profile of oral once-daily ALR-217 differs structurally from the infusion-administered bispecific antibody class, which requires infusion infrastructure and active monitoring for cytokine-release syndrome and neurotoxicity. Cross-trial efficacy comparison is not made; the operational comparison is descriptive of the treatment-administration footprint rather than of clinical effectiveness.
