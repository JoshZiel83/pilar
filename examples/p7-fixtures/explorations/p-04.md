# Explorations — P-04 Clinical Evidence

## 2026-05-15 — durability-claim-grounded-in-current-data

The durability angle does not hold up against the current data cut. Smith_J_2024_Synth-J-Oncol's primary analysis reports the protocol-defined ORR endpoint at week 24 with median follow-up of N months; the Limitations section explicitly states that "long-term follow-up including overall survival has not yet been reported." There is no published time-to-progression curve at a depth that would support a clinically meaningful durability claim today. The pivotal study's design itself does not anchor durability as a primary endpoint, and the supportive analyses available do not report durability of response or PFS at maturity.

The right framing for this engagement: durability is an aspirational statement (see ASP-001 in the engagement's aspirational-statements register), tied to the long-term follow-up readout from the pivotal Phase 2 (Evidence Generation Activity #1 in the briefing). Until that readout, the differentiation argument should anchor on the operational axes — convenience (oral administration, no infusion infrastructure), sequencing flexibility, and tolerability (dominated by manageable cytopenias and infection per Smith_J_2024_Synth-J-Oncol) — rather than on durability. The fallback position recorded in ASP-001 already operationalizes this contingency.

**References explored:** Smith_J_2024_Synth-J-Oncol.

## 2026-05-20 — safety-as-differentiation-axis

Safety reads as a credible primary differentiation axis vs bispecifics for the community-oncologist audience. Smith_J_2024_Synth-J-Oncol reports an ALR-217 AE profile dominated by manageable cytopenias and infection, with no grade ≥3 cardiac signals at the primary analysis. Doe_AB_2023_Synth-Lab-Med (the bispecific A pivotal data summary) reports placeholder rates of cytokine-release syndrome and neurotoxicity that, even read conservatively, sit on a different axis of severity and require monitoring infrastructure (infusion suites, pre-medication, on-call neurology) that community practice settings frequently lack.

The implication for downstream drafting: the AE-profile differentiation is operationally meaningful for community-treated and elderly patients per Strategic Priority 1 (differentiation on convenience and tolerability) and Strategic Priority 3 (credible safety communication). The pillar's `## Scientific Statements` section should carry a dedicated SS for the tolerability/AE profile (SS-02 in the existing fixture pillar) — distinct from the SS-01 efficacy statement — so the safety story has its own evidentiary anchor and can be excerpted into community-facing materials independent of the efficacy claim.

**References explored:** Smith_J_2024_Synth-J-Oncol, Doe_AB_2023_Synth-Lab-Med.
