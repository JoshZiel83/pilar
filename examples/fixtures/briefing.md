# Briefing

## Product

ALR-217 — oral small-molecule inhibitor in development at Aurelis Therapeutics for B-cell malignancies. Once-daily fixed dose, no infusion infrastructure required.

## Indication

Relapsed/refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) in patients ineligible for or progressing after CAR-T cell therapy.

## Lifecycle Stage

Post-Phase 2 single-arm pivotal readout. Anticipated US launch ~18 months out, contingent on regulatory submission timing.

## Audiences

- Practicing hematologist-oncologists in community and academic settings
- Academic key opinion leaders in lymphoma
- Pharmacy & therapeutics committees at large integrated delivery networks
- Specialty pharmacy stakeholders (formulary, prior authorization)

## Strategic Priorities

1. Differentiate ALR-217 from emerging bispecific antibodies on convenience (oral, no infusion), sequencing flexibility, and tolerability — particularly in older patients.
2. Establish optimal positioning in the 3rd-line and beyond setting given the post-CAR-T treatment landscape and the expected influx of bispecifics.
3. Communicate the safety profile credibly to community oncologists, including practical management of expected adverse events and monitoring requirements.

## Competitive Context

Two emerging bispecific antibodies (anti-CD3 × anti-CD20) are competing in the same line of therapy. Both require infusion infrastructure and have notable cytokine-release-syndrome and neurotoxicity profiles. ALR-217's oral administration and distinct AE profile are the primary differentiation axes. One alternative oral agent is also in development with overlapping but not identical patient-selection criteria.
