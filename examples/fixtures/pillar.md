# Pillar: Clinical Evidence

## Strategic Rationale

The Clinical Evidence pillar establishes ALR-217's efficacy and tolerability profile in r/r DLBCL, anchoring the platform's claims of differentiation against bispecific antibodies on convenience and tolerability while acknowledging the limitations of single-arm data. It directly advances Strategic Priorities 1 and 3 of the briefing (differentiation; safety communication) and supplies the evidentiary foundation that the Clinical Value Framework pillar will translate into positioning recommendations.

## Narrative

Pivotal Phase 2 data demonstrate clinically meaningful activity in patients with r/r DLBCL who are ineligible for or have progressed after CAR-T, with a tolerability profile dominated by manageable cytopenias and infections rather than the cytokine-release-syndrome and neurotoxicity patterns characteristic of T-cell engaging therapies. The single-arm design and the absence of a randomized comparator are acknowledged; long-term follow-up and the in-flight real-world evidence study will further characterize durability and effectiveness in routine practice. Subgroup analyses, including elderly patients, support consistent activity across patient profiles likely to be encountered in community practice.

## Scope

In scope: pivotal Phase 2 efficacy and safety findings; subgroup analyses from the pivotal study; mechanistic context relevant to interpreting clinical results; comparative descriptive context from competitor publications.

Out of scope (deferred to other pillars): treatment-sequencing recommendations (Clinical Value Framework), detailed mechanism-of-action exposition (Disease Mechanism / MoA pillar), and broader unmet-need framing (Unmet Need pillar).

## Scientific Statements

### SS-01: Pivotal Phase 2 efficacy in r/r DLBCL

- status: draft
- created: 2026-05-02
- updated: 2026-05-02

**Statement.** ALR-217 demonstrated clinically meaningful activity in adults with r/r DLBCL who were ineligible for or had progressed after CAR-T cell therapy, with response rates supporting its role as an oral option in the 3rd-line and beyond setting.

**Strategic Argument.** Establishes the proof-of-concept evidentiary foundation for the platform. Without supportable efficacy claims grounded in the pivotal study, downstream differentiation and value claims are unanchored. The single-arm design is a known limitation and is hedged appropriately rather than overclaimed.
