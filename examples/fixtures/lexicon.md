# Lexicon

### diffuse large B-cell lymphoma

- preferred: diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) on first use; DLBCL thereafter
- avoid: [diffuse large b-cell lymphoma, DLCL, DLBL]
- definition: An aggressive non-Hodgkin lymphoma of B-cell origin; the most common subtype of non-Hodgkin lymphoma in adults.
- rationale: Match NCCN guideline orthography. Capitalization of "B-cell" with hyphenated lowercase "cell" is conventional in hematology literature.

### overall response rate

- preferred: overall response rate (ORR) on first use; ORR thereafter
- avoid: [response rate (alone), overall response, OR rate]
- definition: The proportion of evaluable patients achieving a complete or partial response per protocol-defined response criteria.
- rationale: Distinguish from "response rate" which is ambiguous. ALR-217 platform consistently uses ORR for the primary efficacy endpoint.

### relapsed/refractory

- preferred: relapsed/refractory (r/r) on first use; r/r thereafter
- avoid: [relapsed and refractory, R/R, refractory/relapsed]
- definition: Disease that has either recurred after prior response (relapsed) or never responded to / progressed during prior therapy (refractory).
- rationale: Slash-separated with lowercase "r/r" matches majority hematology convention.

### CAR-T cell therapy

- preferred: CAR-T cell therapy on first use; CAR-T thereafter
- avoid: [CAR-T therapy, CART, CAR T-cell therapy]
- definition: Chimeric antigen receptor T-cell therapy; an adoptive immunotherapy approach using genetically modified autologous T cells.
- rationale: Hyphenated "CAR-T" with space before "cell" is the NCCN-aligned form; client medical-affairs team uses this convention internally.

### bispecific antibody

- preferred: bispecific antibody on first use; specific molecule names thereafter
- avoid: [bsAb, bispecific (alone, except as adjective), BsAb]
- definition: An antibody engineered to engage two distinct antigens — in r/r DLBCL context, typically anti-CD3 × anti-CD20.
- rationale: Use the full term to maintain accessibility for community-oncologist audiences who may be less familiar with the abbreviation. Switch to molecule names for specificity beyond first reference.
