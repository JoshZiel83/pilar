# Strategic Alignment Report

## Scope

First consolidated draft (cd-001) of the Aurelis ALR-217 platform, comprising five pillars (P-01 Unmet Need, P-02 Disease Mechanism / Pathophysiology, P-03 Mechanism of Action, P-04 Clinical Evidence, P-05 Clinical Value Framework) plus the lexicon and style guide. Strategic Reviewer read the briefing, the roadmap, and the edited consolidated draft (post-Editor pass).

## Briefing Priorities Reviewed

1. Differentiate ALR-217 from emerging bispecific antibodies on convenience, sequencing flexibility, and tolerability — particularly in older patients.
2. Establish optimal positioning in the 3rd-line and beyond setting given the post-CAR-T treatment landscape.
3. Communicate the safety profile credibly to community oncologists, including practical management of expected adverse events and monitoring requirements.

## Findings

### SA-CD1-001

- target: P-04
- priority_affected: Strategic Priority 1 (differentiation vs bispecifics)
- issue: P-04 (Clinical Evidence) is well-developed on efficacy but under-emphasizes the tolerability axis that Strategic Priority 1 hinges on. Tolerability findings appear briefly in SS-01 but lack a dedicated SS that synthesizes the tolerability profile in a way directly comparable (descriptively) against the bispecific antibody safety patterns. P-05 (Clinical Value Framework) leans on tolerability for differentiation but does not cite back into a synthesis statement.
- severity: high
- recommendation: Add a new SS to P-04 that synthesizes the ALR-217 tolerability profile and frames the cross-trial descriptive comparison against bispecific antibody data. Update P-05's positioning argument to cite that new SS.

### SA-CD1-002

- target: deliverable
- priority_affected: Strategic Priority 3 (safety communication to community)
- issue: Safety communication content is distributed across P-04 and P-05 but no pillar takes ownership of the *practical management* dimension (monitoring requirements, AE workflow at community sites). The deliverable as a whole reads as effective for academic audiences but thin for community-oncologist audiences who carry primary responsibility for routine management.
- severity: medium
- recommendation: Either expand P-05's clinical-value framing to include a practical-management subsection, or open scope on a new pillar (P-06 Practical Management) — discuss with client at consolidated-draft review whether the strategic priority warrants a dedicated pillar.

### SA-CD1-003

- target: deliverable
- priority_affected: Strategic Priority 2 (3L+ positioning post-CAR-T)
- issue: Post-CAR-T sequencing is referenced in P-04's open items and in P-05's narrative, but no pillar contains a synthesis statement that maps ALR-217's role in the post-CAR-T treatment landscape. The narrative gap is bridged only descriptively.
- severity: medium
- recommendation: Add a synthesis SS to P-05 (or P-04) that explicitly addresses post-CAR-T sequencing using XYZ_2024_BCL-Guidelines (NCCN excerpt) and the post-CAR-T sequencing review article in the KB.
